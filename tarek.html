Tarek bin Ziyad

About Tarek bin Ziyad

Tarek bin Ziyad was a Muslim commander who led the Umayyad conquest of Visigothic Hispania in 711 AD. He is best known for his leadership in the Battle of Guadalete, which marked the start of Muslim rule in Spain.

Why is He Important?

Tarek bin Ziyad's victory opened the doors for the Islamic presence in Europe, which lasted for centuries. His strategic planning and bravery helped establish Islamic culture and learning in Spain, influencing European history.

Famous Quote

Before the battle, Tarek famously burned his ships, telling his soldiers, "The enemy is in front of you, and the sea is behind you. Where will you go?" This act showed his determination and inspired his troops to fight with courage.

Arabic Writings

Muslims ruled Spain for many years. If you visit Spain today, you will find a lot of Muslim and Arabic historical objects like mosques, Arabic words written on walls, and many other things.

The Benefits Islam Brought to Spain

The agricultural system of the Spanish Muslims was "the most complex, the most scientific, the most perfect, ever devised by the ingenuity of man." Glick supports this by outlining that the Muslims introduced many transformations. A further sign of the Islamic land revolution was that while the rest of Europe crumbled under serfdom and slave labor, the land under Islam was granted to tilling farmers. Under Andalusian Arabs, Joseph McCabe remarked that "great estates tilled by serfs and slaves were rare. Along the course of the Guadalquivir alone, there were 12,000 happy villages."

Technology

Advances in astronomy, surgery, and hydraulic systems like waterwheels, irrigation channels, and reservoirs.
